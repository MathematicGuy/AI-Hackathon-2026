The problem

Customers do not want more specs. They want confidence.

Electronics shopping forces everyday buyers to translate life context into technical filters: BTU, inverter, noise level, capacity, stock, warranty, delivery and promotion rules.

Too many SKU choices

Users compare long lists but still do not know which difference matters.

Spec literacy gap

“Room 18m², quiet, save electricity” must become product constraints.

Low decision trust

Price, promotion and stock change by region; advice must be grounded.

07 · ExperienceExplain the trade-off
AI architecture

AI where it helps, deterministic logic where it protects trust.

Input guardrailScope, language, word limit, unsafe input and basic validation.
Intent + need extractorClassify category, extract budget, constraints, preferences and missing variables.
State mergeSession memory, confirmed assumptions, prior products and clarification history.
Catalog retrievalSearch product data, price, stock, promotion, review and policy sources.
Hard filter + rankingFilter by must-have constraints, then rank by fit-to-need and trade-off role.
Grounded answerTop 3, explanation, sources, uncertainty and next action.
08 · ArchitectureGrounded by design
Trust design

Trust is earned through expectation, explainability and control.

The system should never pretend to know what the data does not show.

Expectation

Make clear: this is a shopping advisor, not a guarantee of availability or final pricing.

Explainability

Show why each product is recommended and which input factors mattered.

Control

Let users change priorities, relax constraints, see sources, or hand off to a human consultant.
